Theory - Free Vibrations

A single degree of freedom system has the equation of motion \begin{equation} M\ddot{r}+C \dot{r}+Kr=R \end{equation} If there is no applied force so that \begin{equation} R=0 \end{equation} then the damped equation of motion is \begin{equation} m\ddot{r}+k\dot{r}=0 \end{equation} This has the solution \begin{equation} \begin{array}{l} r= r_{0}e^{pt}\\ \dot{r}= pr_{0}e^{pt} \\ \ddot{r} = p^{2}r_{0}e^{pt} \end{array} \end{equation} Substituting these back into the equation of motion gives \begin{equation} (p^{2}m+pc+k)r_{0}e^{pt}=0 \end{equation} If the amplitude of vibration is not zero then \begin{equation} (p^{2}m+pc+k)=0 \end{equation} This is called the characteristic equation of the system. It has the solution \begin{equation} p= \frac{1}{2m} \bigg(-c \pm \sqrt[]{c^{2}-4km}\bigg ) \end{equation} For a lightly damped system (in fact it defines a lightly damped system) then \begin{equation} c^{2} < 4km \end{equation} In this case: \begin{equation} p = -\frac{c}{2m} \pm i \frac{1}{2m}\sqrt[]{4km-c^{2}}= \alpha \pm \beta \end{equation} The response is then \begin{equation} r= r_{01} e^{\alpha t} e^{i \beta t} + r_{02}e^{\alpha t}e^{-i \beta t} \end{equation} Where $\beta$ is called the damped natural frequency. \begin{equation} \beta = \sqrt[]{\frac{k}{m}-\frac{c^{2}}{4m^{2}}} \end{equation} The equation of motion can also be written in non-dimensional form by dividing throughout by the mass. \begin{equation} \ddot{r}+\frac{c}{m}\dot{r}+\frac{k}{m}r = \frac{R}{m} \end{equation} or \begin{equation} \ddot{r}+2\omega_{u}\xi\dot{r} +\omega_{u}^{2}r=\frac{R}{m} %xi or zeta ? \end{equation} where $\omega_{u}$= $\sqrt[]{\frac{k}{m}}$ is the undamped resonant frequency $\xi$= $\frac{c}{2\omega_{u}m}$=$\frac{c}{2 \ \sqrt[]{km}}$=$\frac{c}{c_{0}}$ is the damping factor or damping ratio. $c_{0}$=$2 \ \sqrt[]{km}$ is the critical damping value. \begin{equation} \beta= \omega_{u} \bigg( 1- \frac{c^{2}}{4km} \bigg)^{1/2} = \omega_{u}\big(1-\xi^{2}\big)^{1/2} \end{equation} and $\alpha$ is $\alpha$=$\omega_{u}\xi$

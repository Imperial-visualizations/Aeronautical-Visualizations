Theory

Vibration frequency

The equation of motion for the dynamic system can be written as following, where damping is ignored for simplicity:

$$\begin{bmatrix}m & 0\\0 & I\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \ddot{r} \\ \ddot{θ} \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix}k & -kl\\-kl & k_θ+kl^2\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}r\\θ\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}R\\Θ\end{bmatrix} $$

A normal mode of an oscillating system is a pattern of motion in which all parts of the system move sinusoidally with the same frequency and with a fixed phase relation. The free motion described by the normal modes takes place at the fixed vibration frequencies. In order to compute the vibration frequencies for the two mode shapes in this dynamic system, responses are assumed to be periodic. The displacement vector can be expressed as $\begin{bmatrix}r\\θ\end{bmatrix} = \cos(\omega t)\cdot\begin{bmatrix}r_0\\θ_0\end{bmatrix}$ and acceleration vector is $\begin{bmatrix}r\\θ\end{bmatrix} = -\omega^2\cdot\cos(\omega t)\cdot\begin{bmatrix}r_0\\θ_0\end{bmatrix}$, where $r_0, \theta_0$ are constants. Replacing back to equation of motions and set forces $(R, \Theta)$ to zero, a matrix can be written to describe free vibration: $$ \begin{bmatrix}k-\omega^2m & -kl\\-kl & k_\theta+kl^2-\omega^2I\end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{bmatrix}r_0\\θ_0\end{bmatrix} = 0 $$

Therefore, the vibration frequency can be found by setting the determinant of the matrix to zero: $$ det\begin{bmatrix}k-\omega^2m & -kl\\-kl & k_\theta+kl^2-\omega^2I\end{bmatrix} = 0 $$ By calculation, the vibration frequencies for two mode shapes are: $$ \omega^2 = \frac{[m(k_\theta+kl^2)+kI]\pm\sqrt{[m(k_\theta+kl^2)-kI]^2+4m^2k_\theta kl^2}}{2mI}$$

The flexibility matrix can be found using the load method. Consider a coordinate $z$ pointing downwards from the centre of the nacelle. Let $r$ be the first degree of freedom and $\theta$ be the second.

The bending moments due to unit loads at DOFs 1 and 2 are, respectively, $M_1 = z$ and $M_2 = 1$ (this follows the right hand convention for the sign of the moment; following any other convention for \emph{both} expressions would be equally correct).

The flexibility terms can be obtained as: $$ F_{11} = \frac{1}{EI} \int_0^\ell \! M_1 M_1 \, \mathrm{d}z= \frac{1}{EI} \int_0^\ell \! z^2 \, \mathrm{d}z = \frac{1}{3} \frac{\ell^3}{EI} \, , $$ $$ F_{12} = \frac{1}{EI} \int_0^\ell \! M_1 M_2 \, \mathrm{d}z= \frac{1}{EI} \int_0^\ell \! z \, \mathrm{d}z = \frac{1}{2} \frac{\ell^2}{EI} \, , $$ $$ F_{22} = \frac{1}{EI} \int_0^\ell \! M_2 M_2 \, \mathrm{d}z= \frac{1}{EI} \int_0^\ell \! 1 \, \mathrm{d}z = \frac{\ell}{EI} \, . $$

This leads to the flexibility matrix $$ \mathbf{F} = \frac{\ell}{EI} \begin{bmatrix} \ell^2 / 3 & \ell /2 \\ \ell /2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{\ell}{6EI} \begin{bmatrix} 2\ell^2 & 3\ell \\ 3\ell & 6 \end{bmatrix}. $$

The stiffness matrix is the inverse of the flexibility matrix. The determinant of the flexibility matrix is $\Delta = \left( \frac{\ell}{6EI} \right) ^2 \left( 12\ell^2 - 9 \ell^2 \right) = \frac{\ell^4}{12 \left( EI \right)^2}$.

The stiffness matrix comes then as $$\mathbf{K} = \frac{2EI}{\ell^3} \begin{bmatrix} 6 & -3 \ell \\ -3 \ell & 2\ell^2 \end{bmatrix} . $$

Given that the coordinates are attached to the centre of mass, the mass matrix must be diagonal with leading diagonal terms $m$ and $J$ respectively. This can be explained for instance by considering the inertia force associated with DoF $r \space (m\ddot{r})$ and the inertia moment associated with DoF $\theta \space (J\ddot{\theta})$; for these generalised forces to appear correctly in the equations of motion, the mass matrix must be as stated. Finally, the equations of motion are: $$\begin{bmatrix}m & 0\\0 & J\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \ddot{r} \\ \ddot{θ} \end{bmatrix} + \frac{2EI}{\ell^3}\begin{bmatrix}6 & -3\ell\\-3\ell & 2\ell^2\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}r\\θ\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}R\\Θ\end{bmatrix} $$

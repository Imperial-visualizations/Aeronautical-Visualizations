The equations of motion can be expressed as: $$M\ddot{r} + Kr = R$$

Using $r=r_0 \cos(\Omega t),$ the second derivative is: $$\ddot{r} = -\Omega^2 r_0 \cos(\Omega t).$$

Replacing in the equations of motion and solving for $r_0$: $$r_0 = (K - \Omega^2 M)^{-1} R_0.$$

Expanding and using $\Omega^2 = \omega^2 = k/m$ leads to: $$ \begin{bmatrix} r_{10} \\ r_{20} \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{\Delta} k \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 2 \\ 2 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} R_0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{\Delta} k\begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 2R_0 \end{bmatrix}$$ where $\Delta = \det(K - \Omega^2M) = -4k^2.$

Displacement $r_1$ is zero, even though there is a load applied to DOF 1. The converse is ture for $r_2.$ The top mass is in free vibration. The force of the top spring on the bottom mass equals at any time the applied force. This type of arrangement is known as a vibrations absorber.

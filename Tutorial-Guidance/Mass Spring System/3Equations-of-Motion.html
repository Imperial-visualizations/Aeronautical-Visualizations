Initial Setup

Force equilibrium of mass $m$ leads to: $$ m\ddot{r_1}(t) + kr_1(t) = R_0 \cos(\Omega t). $$

The second derivative of the assumed displacement is $\ddot{r_1}(t) = -\Omega^2 r_{10} \cos(\Omega t).$

Replacing in the equation of motion leads to: $$r_{10} = \frac{R_0/k}{1 - (\frac{\Omega}{\omega})^2}.$$ If $\Omega \equiv \omega$, then the response tends to infinity, as expected for periodic loading at the resonant frequency and without damping.

Revised Setup

Equilibrium of forces for the bottom mass leads to: $$-m\ddot{r_1} + 2k(r_2 - r_1) - k r_1 + R_1 = 0,$$ and, for the top mass, $$-2m\ddot{r_2} - 2k(r_2 - r_1) = 0.$$

The equations of motion for the dynamic system can be written as: $$m \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0\\0 & 1\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \ddot{r_1} \\ \ddot{r_2} \end{bmatrix} + \frac{2\sqrt{2}EA}{\ell} \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0\\0 & 1\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} r_1 \\ r_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}R_1\\R_2\end{bmatrix} $$

A vibration mode of an oscillating system is a pattern of motion in which all parts of the system move sinusoidally with the same frequency. In order to compute the vibration frequencies for the free motion described by two vibration modes of this dynamic system, the responses are assumed to be periodic. Thus, the displacement vector and acceleration vector can be expressed as $$\begin{bmatrix}r_1\\r_2\end{bmatrix} = \cos(\omega t) \begin{bmatrix}r_{10}\\r_{20}\end{bmatrix}$$ $$\begin{bmatrix} \ddot{r_1}\\ \ddot{r_2}\end{bmatrix} = -\omega^2 \cos(\omega t) \begin{bmatrix}r_{10}\\r_{20}\end{bmatrix}$$ where $r_{10}$ and $r_{20}$ are constants.

Replacing back to the equations of motion and setting the forces $(R_1, R_2)$ to zero for free vibrations: $$\omega^2 m \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0\\0 & 1\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}r_{10}\\r_{20}\end{bmatrix} = \frac{2\sqrt{2}EA}{\ell} \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0\\0 & 1\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}r_{10}\\r_{20}\end{bmatrix} $$ This is an eigenvalue problem where $\omega^2$ is the eigenvalue and $\begin{bmatrix}r_{10}\\r_{20}\end{bmatrix}$ the associated eigenvector.

Solving for eigenvalues and eigenvectors: $$\begin{bmatrix} \frac{2\sqrt{2}EA}{\ell} - \omega^2 m & 0\\0 & \frac{2\sqrt{2}EA}{\ell} - \omega^2 m\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}r_{10}\\r_{20}\end{bmatrix} = 0$$ Therefore, the natural vibration frequencies can be found by setting the determinant of the matrix to zero: $$ \det \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2\sqrt{2}EA}{\ell} - \omega^2 m & 0\\0 & \frac{2\sqrt{2}EA}{\ell} - \omega^2 m\end{bmatrix} = 0 $$ Finally, the square of the vibration frequencies for the two mode shapes are: $$ \omega_i^2 = \frac{2\sqrt{2}EA}{m\ell} $$ The two degrees of freedom are uncoupled. Both natural frequencies are $ \omega = \sqrt{\frac{2\sqrt{2}EA}{m\ell}} \space$.

The first thing to note is that the vertical and horizontal bars do not play any role in this structure as their ends are fixed. Let's define for simplicity $k=2\sqrt{2}AE/L$. Applying the unit displacement method:

  • $r_1=1$ and $r_2=0$. The bars on the left extend by $1/ \sqrt{2}$ and the ones on the right contract by the same amount. From equilibrium, this leads to $R_1=K_{11}=4 \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} AE/(\sqrt{2}L/2)=k$ and from symmetry it is trivial that $R_2=K_{12}=0$.
  • $r_1=0$ and $r_2=1$. From symmetry (of the stiffness matrix), this leads to $R_1=K_{21}=0$ and, from symmetry (of the problem wrt the DOF), $R_2=K_{22}=k$.

Hence the stiffness matrix is: $$ \mathbf{K}= k \begin{bmatrix} 1& 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} $$

Since the engine can be represented as a point mass: $$ M = m\begin{bmatrix}1 & 0\\0 & 1\end{bmatrix} $$

Hence the equations of equilibrium are: $$m \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0\\0 & 1\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \ddot{r_1} \\ \ddot{r_2} \end{bmatrix} + \frac{2\sqrt{2}EA}{\ell} \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0\\0 & 1\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} r_1 \\ r_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}R_1\\R_2\end{bmatrix} $$
